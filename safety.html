Child safety & wellbeing

Before anything else, your child is safe here.

Safety isn't a policy we keep in a folder. It's the quiet, everyday work that lets your child explore, take small risks and grow, knowing the adults around them are watching out for them.

Like every parent in Australia, you've heard the recent news about safety in early childhood settings. We understand exactly how that feels, because we feel it too. It's why we welcome the stronger national protections introduced across 2025 and 2026, and why we hold ourselves to a standard higher than the minimum the law requires.

Here is precisely how we keep your child safe, in plain language, with nothing hidden.

Every single day

The safeguards that never switch off.

Every adult is checked

Every educator, staff member and regular visitor holds a current, verified NSW Working With Children Check. No exceptions, and we monitor them so none lapse.

Qualified, familiar faces

Your child is cared for by trained early childhood educators who stay with us. Low turnover means consistent, trusted relationships, not a rotating cast of strangers.

Secure arrivals & departures

Children are signed in and out, the gate is supervised, and only the adults you authorise can collect your child. We confirm, every time.

Always supervised

We meet and aim to exceed regulated educator-to-child ratios, with active supervision planned across every space, indoors and out.

A child-safe culture

We embed the National Principles for Child Safe Organisations and the NSW Child Safe Standards, where children are listened to, and every adult knows how to recognise and report a concern.

Trained to respond

Our educators hold current first aid, CPR, asthma and anaphylaxis training, and understand their mandatory reporting responsibilities.

Every child belongs

We care for each child as a unique individual and support children of all abilities and backgrounds to take part fully. Inclusion isn't an add-on here, it's how we plan every day.

Mandatory reporting, understood

All educators know their legal duty to report concerns about a child's safety, and act on it, working with the right authorities whenever a child's wellbeing is involved.

The 2025–26 national reforms

Stronger protections, and we welcome every one.

Australia has strengthened the rules for early childhood education. Here's what's changed, and what it means for your child at St Luke's.

Phones & photos, tightly controlled

Under the new National Law, personal mobile devices are restricted while educators work directly with children. Only secure, service-issued devices are used to take photos, with your authorisation, and images are stored and disposed of under a strict policy.

Clear digital & online-safety policies

From September 2025, services must have policies governing how children's images are taken, used, stored and destroyed, how consent is managed, and how any surveillance is handled. Ours are documented and available to you.

Mandatory child-safety training

A new national child-safety training program is being rolled out for everyone working in early education. Our team completes ongoing child-protection training as part of how we work, not a one-off tick-box.

A national educator register

Australia is introducing a national register of early childhood educators to lift accountability across the sector. We fully support it and the transparency it brings to families everywhere.

We continue to meet the requirements of the National Quality Framework and stay current with each reform as it commences. If you'd ever like to read a specific policy, simply ask, we'll happily share it.

Open door, always

If something's on your mind, tell us.

The safest centres are the ones where families feel completely comfortable raising anything, big or small. You can speak to your child's educator or our Director at any time, and we'll listen properly.

We also have clear, written processes for handling concerns and complaints, and obligations to report and act when a child's safety is involved. You'll never be brushed aside.

Complaints & feedback

If something's wrong, here's exactly what to do.

Raising a concern is always welcome, never held against you or your child. You have several ways to be heard.

Talk to us first

Speak with your child's educator or our Director, in person, by phone on (02) 4261 4122, or by email. Most things are resolved quickly this way.

We look into it

We take every concern seriously, follow our documented complaints process, and keep you informed of what we're doing and when.

Escalate if you need to

If a concern involves a child's safety, we're obligated to act and report. You can also contact the NSW Department of Education's early childhood regulator directly. [CONFIRM: add regulator contact / link, e.g. NSW ECEC 1800 619 113]
