Our philosophy

Every child, a unique creation, known and nurtured.

What we believe about children, families and learning, and how it shapes every ordinary, extraordinary day.

Our vision

Christ-centred. Child-focused. Built on collaborative partnerships.

We serve one another, our families and our community through high-quality teaching and a commitment to always getting better. Through God's love and grace, we build meaningful, trusting relationships with everyone who walks alongside us.

It's a simple idea, lived out carefully: when children feel safe, known and loved, they're free to learn.

What we believe

The convictions behind the everyday.

The child

Each child is unique and a creation of God, with a right to excellent education and care, regardless of difference. We support every child's interests, strengths and stage.

Families

The family is the most influential part of a child's life, and the central place where children are nurtured and taught. We see ourselves as partners, never replacements.

Communication

Good learning is a two-way conversation between school and home, built on trust and respect. We keep families genuinely in the loop.

Cultural diversity

Every child's uniqueness is woven into a program that treats children as capable, competent learners, fostering resilience, self-esteem and confidence.

Community

We contribute actively to Dapto and connect families to the local services, people and support that strengthen family life.

Relationships

The partnership between children, families, community and educators is paramount to each child's growth, so we tend it with care.

Play & environment

Learning that grows from play.

Through a stimulating, play-centred curriculum we encourage exploration, investigation and a lifelong desire to learn. Children's play is a meaningful expression of who they are and how they connect with others.

Our natural learning environment invites participation at every stage of development, so children master new skills while being gently supported, never rushed. These are the practices the Early Years Learning Framework recognises as central to how young children grow.

Curriculum

Local hands shape the learning.

We align with national standards, then make the curriculum come alive locally, shaped by teachers, parents, community and the children themselves, within the national framework.

Our educators

Trained to exceed, not just meet.

Every educator holds current early-childhood training, and we aim to exceed the minimum national requirements. Our team supports, scaffolds and nurtures each child to reach their full potential, with intentional teaching that builds the whole child.
