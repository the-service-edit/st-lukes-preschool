What happens next?

After you apply, here's every step.

No surprises, and no guessing. This is exactly how a waiting-list application becomes a place at St Luke's.

We confirm within 7 days

We request two documents

Places offered by priority

Positions are offered using the Australian Government Priority of Access Guidelines together with your application date.

Welcome & orientation

When a place is offered, we help you settle your child in gently, with an orientation visit before their first day.

The Priority of Access Guidelines are national rules that help services offer places fairly, for example prioritising children at risk or families with additional needs. If you'd like to understand where your family sits, just ask, we're happy to talk it through.

Questions parents ask

Before you apply, the honest answers.

When should we join the waiting list?

As early as you can. Because places are offered partly by application date, adding your child now helps your position, even if you're planning a year or two ahead. It's free, and there's no commitment.

Can we visit before applying?

Absolutely, and we'd encourage it. You can arrange a visit at any time, with no obligation to enrol. Many families visit and join the waiting list at the same time.

How do you help children settle in?

Gently, and at your child's pace. Before the first day we arrange an orientation visit so faces, spaces and routines feel familiar. Our educators stay for the long term, so the person welcoming your child at the gate is someone they come to know and trust.

What should my child bring?

[CONFIRM: what to bring, e.g. a sun hat, drink bottle, spare clothes, morning tea and lunch, in a named bag] We'll give you a simple checklist before your child starts.

How does the preschool prepare children for school?

School readiness grows out of everyday play: confidence, curiosity, independence, early literacy and numeracy, and the social skills of sharing, listening and problem-solving. Our program follows the Early Years Learning Framework and gently builds the whole child, so they start school settled and ready to learn.

How are places allocated?

From the waiting list, using the Australian Government Priority of Access Guidelines together with your application date. Applying early helps your position.
