The brief we set ourselves

Evolve, don't replace.

St Luke's has something most preschools would envy: seventy years of trust in one community. The job was never to invent a new brand. It was to remove the friction and dated edges that were quietly undercutting that trust online, and let the warmth of the place finally show through.

We kept your logo, your Christian values, your four pillars, your history and your real photography. We modernised the structure, the typography, the hierarchy and the path a parent takes toward an enquiry.

The honest audit

What we found on the old site.

What already builds trust, kept

  • Exceeding NQS rating, your single strongest credential. Now front and centre, not buried.
  • 70-year history & church connection, rare proof of stability.
  • Genuine photography of real children and play, far more convincing than stock.
  • The four pillars and Christ-centred values, kept, reworded warmly.
  • Acknowledgement of Country, retained and given a calm, respectful home.

What was hurting credibility

  • Placeholder text left live, "Whatever it is, the way you tell your story…" on the Team page reads as unfinished.
  • "Welcome to St Luke's Preschool!" plus generic greetings, dated and forgettable.
  • Inconsistent type, mixed cases like "oUR pHILOSOPHY" signal neglect.
  • A single 2020 blog post as "latest", suggests the site is abandoned.
  • Broken / empty images in the hero and gallery.

What felt dated, modernised

  • Boxy, stacked Squarespace sections → editorial, asymmetric compositions.
  • Cramped spacing → generous whitespace that feels calm and premium.
  • Small, dense text → large, readable editorial typography.
  • Icon-heavy clip-art feel → restrained, natural palette and photography-led design.

What created friction for parents

  • No clear primary action. The only path was a single waiting-list form, with no obvious next step for new families.
  • Nine navigation items (Blog, Videos, St Luke's @ Home…) split attention.
  • No reassurance about cost, ages or what a day looks like.
  • Weak mobile experience, most parents browse on a phone at night.
The decisions that matter

Seven changes, each with a reason.

01

One clear path to enrol

Every page now funnels gently toward a single, obvious action, joining the waiting list, with "Enrol Now" carrying the weight and your contact details never more than a tap away.

02

Led with proof in five seconds

Exceeding NQS, ages 3–5, Est. 1953, Christ-centred, Dapto location, all visible the instant the page loads, building trust before a word is read.

03

Simplified navigation to five items

Home, Our Approach, Our Educators, Our Story, Contact. Blog and Videos can return later as a "News" section once there's fresh content, an empty blog hurts more than no blog.

04

Made the educators the hero

Parents choose people before programs. A dedicated, photography-led team page does more for enrolments than any feature list.

05

Added a "daily rhythm" so parents can picture the day

The single biggest unspoken question, "what will my child actually do all day?", now has a calm, visual answer.

06

Rewrote every word in an educator's voice

Warm, plain, Australian. No "We pride ourselves", no jargon. It reads like a person who loves children, because that's who parents want to meet.

07

Rebuilt for mobile, speed, SEO and accessibility

Mobile-first layouts, a sticky "Enrol Now" button, proper heading structure, schema markup for local search, alt text and keyboard accessibility throughout.

08

Made child safety a headline, not fine print

After the national conversation about safety in early childhood, parents are anxious. We added a dedicated Child Safety page and a homepage section that plainly explain your safeguards and your alignment with the 2025–26 reforms (device and photography restrictions, child-safe standards, Working With Children Checks, mandatory training). It turns a parent's biggest fear into a reason to choose you.

To make it fully real

A short list of what only you can provide.

Educator details. The team cards use placeholders like [Name]. Send each educator's name, role, qualification and one warm sentence, plus a relaxed photo, and the people page comes alive.
Two or three real testimonials. The quote cards are marked as samples. A few genuine lines from current families (your Google or Facebook reviews are a great source) will outperform anything we could write.
A handful of high-resolution photos. The current images are reused from your existing site and look great here. When you next have a warm, sunny day, a short documentary-style photo session, children exploring, educators alongside them, would lift the hero imagery further.
Current fees & subsidy details and live form connections (waiting list, contact) to wire up before launch.
Please verify the Child Safety page before it goes live. The safeguards described reflect the current NSW and national requirements for an Exceeding-rated service and the 2025–26 reforms. Before publishing, confirm each statement matches your actual policies and practices (for example, your photography and device policy, and whether CCTV is in use), so every word is accurate to St Luke's. We've deliberately avoided claiming anything you haven't told us, such as installed CCTV.
